Tax Treatment & Regulatory Disclaimer

Government Subscribers (Unchanged): At regular exit (age 60), minimum 40% of corpus must be used to purchase an annuity. Up to 60% may be withdrawn as a tax-free lump sum under Section 10(12A) of the IT Act, 1961.

Private / Non-Government Subscribers (Updated Dec 2025): Per PFRDA's Amendment Regulations 2025 (effective December 2025), the mandatory annuity has been reduced from 40% to 20%. Up to 80% may now be withdrawn — however, only 60% is tax-exempt u/s 10(12A). The additional 20% (between 60–80%) is taxable at the subscriber's applicable income tax slab rate. Subscribers with corpus ≤ ₹8 lakh may withdraw 100% without purchasing any annuity.

Annuity Income: Monthly pension received is fully taxable as "Income from Other Sources" at the subscriber's slab rate. Senior citizens may claim a deduction of up to ₹50,000 p.a. on interest income u/s 80TTB.

Premature Exit: Exiting before age 60 — minimum 80% must be invested in annuity; only 20% may be withdrawn as lump sum (taxable). Exception: corpus ≤ ₹5 lakh may be withdrawn in full.
