Tax Treatment & Regulatory Disclaimer

Lump Sum Withdrawal: At maturity (age 60), up to 60% of the accumulated NPS corpus withdrawn as a lump sum is fully tax-exempt under Section 10(17A) of the Income-tax Act, 1961. The remaining corpus (minimum 40%) must be compulsorily utilised to purchase an annuity from a PFRDA-empanelled ASP.

Annuity Income: The monthly pension received is fully taxable as "Income from Other Sources" at the subscriber's applicable slab rate in the year of receipt. Senior citizens may claim a deduction of up to ₹50,000 p.a. on interest income u/s 80TTB; TDS provisions apply as per prevailing Income-tax rules.

Premature Exit: For exit before 60 years of age, a minimum of 80% of the corpus must be invested in an annuity; only 20% may be withdrawn as a lump sum (taxable as income in the year of withdrawal). Death benefit rules follow the nomination instructions registered with the CRA.
